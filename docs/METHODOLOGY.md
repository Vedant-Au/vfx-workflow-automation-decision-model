# Methodology

## Process diagnosis

The case maps the client-to-delivery pipeline and the parallel weekly-reporting process. Bottlenecks are assessed through the Theory of Constraints: the limiting condition is delayed information synchronisation across spreadsheets, production tracking and email.

## Decision model

Each option receives a 0-100 score against six criteria. The overall score is:

`model score = sum(criterion score × criterion weight)`

The fixed weights reflect near-term decision priorities. A 10,000-draw Dirichlet sensitivity test replaces them with random non-negative weights that sum to one. The win rate shows whether the recommendation depends on a narrow weighting choice.

## Evidence boundary

The criteria and relative scores are a portfolio enhancement derived from the case SWOT and workflow evidence. They are not measured implementation results or vendor benchmarks. A real procurement decision should replace them through stakeholder scoring, technical discovery and commercial quotes.
